## STEP 2: Qualifying a Lead (30 seconds)

Before reaching out, check:

- [ ] Are they at a **company** (not a solo developer or student)?
- [ ] Company has **20+ employees** (enough to need org-wide adoption)?
- [ ] They've posted about **using Claude Code at work** (not just personal)?
- [ ] Their role suggests **decision-making ability** (eng lead, CTO, VP, head of platform)?

If 3/4 = reach out. If 2/4 = maybe. If 1/4 = skip.

## STEP 4: The Discovery Call Script (15-20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
> "Thanks for making time. Quick context — I used to run an enterprise chatbot company. Now I'm exploring how teams scale AI coding tools like Claude Code across their organisation. I'm in pure learning mode, no product to sell. I have about 6 questions — should take 15 minutes. Cool?"

### Questions (12 min)

**Q1: Current state** (2 min)
> "How does your team use Claude Code today? Is it individual developers doing their own thing, or is there an org-wide approach?"

*Listen for: how many people, any standardisation, any shared config*

**Q2: Pain points** (3 min)
> "What's the hardest part about getting Claude Code working well across the team? Where do people get stuck?"

*Listen for: inconsistency, knowledge sharing, onboarding, security concerns, cost control*

**Q3: Workflows** (2 min)
> "Are there specific workflows or processes that you wish Claude Code could handle better? Things that are repetitive across the team?"

*Listen for: code review, deployment, documentation, testing, onboarding — these are future skills*

**Q4: Skills/plugins** (2 min)
> "Have you built any custom Claude Code skills, MCP servers, or Cowork plugins? Or is everything out of the box?"

*Listen for: custom work = sophisticated user. Nothing custom = earlier stage*

**Q5: The money question** (2 min)
> "If someone could come in, map your team's key workflows, and turn them into reusable Claude Code skills that everyone could use — would that be valuable? Would you pay for it?"

*Listen for: "yes" = strong signal. "We'd do it ourselves" = weak signal. "What would that cost?" = VERY strong signal*

**Q6: Who decides** (1 min)
> "If you were going to bring in outside help for this, who would make that decision? You, your manager, your CTO?"

*Listen for: the actual buyer title and budget authority*

### Closing (2 min)
> "This was incredibly helpful. Two quick things:
> 1. Can I follow up in a few weeks if I build something based on what I'm hearing?
> 2. Do you know anyone else whose team is trying to scale Claude Code? I'd love an intro."

*Always ask for the referral. This is how you get from 5 conversations to 15.*

## STEP 7: What Good Looks Like vs. Red Flags

### Strong Signals (business is real)
- 3+ people say "I'd pay for that"
- Someone asks "when can you start?"
- Someone asks "how much would that cost?" (unprompted)
- Referrals flow easily — people know others with the same problem
- The pain is specific: "we waste 2 hours per developer per week on inconsistent Claude Code setups"

### Weak Signals (pivot or dig deeper)
- "That's interesting" (interest is not demand)
- "We'd probably just figure it out internally" (no willingness to pay)
- "Let me think about it" (polite no)
- No one can name the specific pain point
- Everyone's experience is different — no pattern emerges

### Red Flags (probably not a business)
- Can't find 15 people who use Claude Code at their company
- Nobody responds to outreach (the community doesn't exist yet)
- Everyone says "it's too early, we're still experimenting"
- The buyer is always someone else ("talk to my CTO" → CTO says "talk to the eng leads")
